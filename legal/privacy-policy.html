UK GDPR notice

Last updated: 17 March 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how SwiftBee Ltd processes personal data when you use IndieRanks. It is intended to help users understand what data we collect, why we use it, who may receive it, and what rights are available.

1. Who we are

For the purposes of applicable data protection law, including the UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018, SwiftBee Ltd is the controller of personal data processed through IndieRanks.

If you have privacy questions, contact us at support@swiftbee.co.uk.

2. What we collect

Account and sign-in data

  • name, email address, and basic profile information from email sign-up or supported sign-in providers;
  • account identifiers, authentication provider identifiers, and account status information;
  • browser storage data used to remember theme preferences and keep core account sessions working.

Project and profile data

  • project names, founder names, categories, descriptions, taglines, logos, website links, and timestamps;
  • submitted metrics, growth percentages, bracket-related metadata, and founder or project profile content;
  • public listing status, moderation state, and verification status labels.

Verification and support data

  • verification types, proof URLs, dashboard notes, repository references, and related review information;
  • email addresses, messages, and support history when you contact us.

Analytics and technical data

  • device, browser, page, approximate usage, and event data collected through analytics or platform tooling;
  • security, logging, and request metadata used to operate, troubleshoot, and protect the Service.

3. How we use personal data

We use personal data to:

  • create and manage accounts and authenticate users;
  • store, review, moderate, and publish project submissions and founder profiles;
  • operate bracket views, rankings, search, filters, and verification workflows;
  • respond to support requests and communicate about your account or listings;
  • measure product usage, improve features, fix bugs, and understand performance;
  • detect abuse, protect the Service, enforce our Terms, and comply with legal obligations.

4. Lawful bases

We typically rely on one or more of the following lawful bases under the UK GDPR:

  • Contract: where processing is necessary to provide IndieRanks features you request, such as account access, submissions, or public listing management;
  • Legitimate interests: where processing is reasonably necessary to run, improve, secure, support, and defend the Service, provided those interests are not overridden by your rights and interests;
  • Legal obligation: where we need to keep records, respond to lawful requests, prevent fraud, or satisfy legal duties;
  • Consent: where consent is required by law for a specific activity.

5. Public leaderboard data

Some IndieRanks features are public by design. If a project is listed, information such as the project name, founder name, category, description, website link, logo, headline metrics, growth figures, and verification status may appear publicly on leaderboard, project, or founder pages.

Public data may be indexed, cached, or archived by third parties. If you want a listing corrected, reviewed, or removed, contact support@swiftbee.co.uk.

6. Sharing and processors

We may share personal data with service providers that help us run IndieRanks, including providers for:

  • hosting, cloud storage, authentication, and analytics;
  • email delivery, support handling, backups, and abuse prevention;
  • professional advisers, auditors, insurers, or legal compliance where reasonably necessary.

We may also disclose information where reasonably necessary to enforce our rights, prevent harm, respond to lawful requests, or in connection with a merger, financing, or sale of assets.

7. International transfers

Some of our service providers may process personal data outside the UK. Where that happens, we aim to use appropriate safeguards recognised under data protection law, such as adequacy regulations, standard contractual clauses, or equivalent transfer protections offered by the relevant provider.

8. Retention

We keep personal data for as long as necessary for the purposes described in this Policy, including to provide the Service, keep records, resolve disputes, maintain security, and comply with legal obligations.

  • account data is generally kept while your account remains active and for a reasonable period afterwards;
  • project listings, founder profiles, and moderation records may be retained while they are live or needed for audit, abuse prevention, and backup purposes;
  • support messages and logs may be retained for service improvement, support history, and legal defence.

9. Your rights

Depending on your location and the circumstances, you may have the right to:

  • request access to your personal data;
  • request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data;
  • request deletion of certain data;
  • object to or restrict certain processing;
  • request portability of data you provided to us where legally applicable;
  • withdraw consent where we rely on consent;
  • complain to the UK Information Commissioner's Office if you believe your rights have been infringed.

10. Cookies and similar technologies

IndieRanks uses essential browser storage and similar technologies to remember basic preferences, such as theme settings, and to support sign-in or session-related behaviour where enabled.

We may also use analytics tools or identifiers to understand how the Service is used, diagnose issues, and improve performance. You can control cookies or storage through your browser settings, but blocking essential storage may affect how the Service works.

11. Security

We use reasonable technical and organisational measures to help protect personal data. However, no system is completely secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security of information transmitted or stored through the internet.

12. Contact and complaints

For privacy requests or questions, contact support@swiftbee.co.uk.

If you are not satisfied with how we handle your request, you may also complain to the UK Information Commissioner's Office.

If we materially change this Privacy Policy, we will update the date at the top of the page and may provide additional notice where appropriate.
